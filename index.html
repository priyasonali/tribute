Walt Disney

"If you can dream it, you can do it."

Life events of Walt Disney:

  • December 5, 1901 - Walt Disney was born in Chicago.
  • 1911 - Got introduced to the world of vaudeville and motion pictures, by his friend Walter.
  • 1917 - Became the cartoonist of the school newspaper, drawing patriotic pictures about World War.
  • September 1918 - Joined the Red Cross as an ambulance driver.
  • 1920, - Disney and Iwerks started their own business.
  • May 1921 - Established Laugh-O-Gram Studio.
  • 1923 - Laugh-O-Gram Studio went into bankruptcy.
  • 1923 - Disney moved to Hollywood and signed the contract for Alice Comedies.
  • 1923 - Disney and his brother Roy formed the Disney Brothers Studio which later became The Walt Disney Company to produce the films.
  • 1925 - Disney got married to an ink artist Lillian Bounds.
  • May 1928 - Mickey Mouse first appeared.
  • 1933 - Disney produced The Three Little Pigs, a film described by the media historian Adrian Danks as "the most successful short animation of all time".
  • 1948 - Disney initiated a series of popular live-action nature films, titled True-Life Adventures, with Seal Island the first; the film won the Academy Award in the Best Short Subject (Two-Reel) category.
  • 1954 - The Disneyland got funding.
  • 1960 - Disney developed plans for a ski resort in Mineral King, a glacial valley in California's Sierra Nevada.
  • 1960 - Disney was inducted to the Hollywood Walk of Fame with two stars, one for motion pictures and the other for his television work.
  • 1966 - Disney cultivated businesses willing to sponsor EPCOT.
  • November 1966 - He was diagnosed with lung cancer.
  • December 15, 1966 - He died of circulatory collapse caused by lung cancer.
  • 1978 - Mickey Mouse was given his own star for motion pictures.
